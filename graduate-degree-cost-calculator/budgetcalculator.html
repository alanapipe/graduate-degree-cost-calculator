How Much Does It Cost To Become A Journalist in New York City?

By Alana Pipe

Pauliina Sinauer, an international student of journalism, works on a story. Photo by Alana Pipe.

The price of a journalism degree in New York can range anywhere from around $17,000 to just under $75,000. The pricey costs of living combined with the opportunity cost of working a full time job (which in journalism is never guaranteed to pay six figures) makes pursuing a journalism degree a big decision.

“Between paying rent, student loans, and wanting to be involved in the restaurant world without racking up a bunch of debt, it’s difficult to be in grad school in this city,” said Emily Ziemski, a former chef and aspiring food writer pursuing studies at the CUNY Graduate School of Journalism. While it’s hardly news living in New York City is more expensive than the rest of the country, journalists’ salaries have largely remained the same, according to data collected by the Bureau of Labor Statistics.

Despite the financial stresses of students, however, some seasoned journalists remain optimistic about beginning a career in the industry. “Everybody [at CUNY] gets a job, and the average starting salary over the past couple of years is a shade under $60,000,” said Tim Harper, a professor at the CUNY Graduate School of Journalism, in an email.” All this considered, the financial burden of beginning a journalism degree will boil down to the choices you make before and during your degree.
